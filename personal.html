Volunteer, hiker, and tennis player

I have been playing tennis since I was seven years old. I have always been interested in playing tennis as many members were also avid tennis players and excelled at the sport. It was first a hobby for me but I quickly developed a desire to have competition, and enjoyed the battles of being out on the court. On the court, you learn from a young age that you are in the fight alone in a match without any help. This helped me develop the ability to work alone an to figure out my problems by myself if I run into them. While tennis is very much a team sport at the colleg level, you have your own battle each match and you do not have the luxury of blaming someone else when you have a bad day. These are lessons that I believe I can carry with me into the workforce, and help contribute in many ways.

Volunteer work

I have greatly enjoyed the esprit de corps and giving spirit of my tennis team, engaging in cooperative efforts such as community trash-pick up initiatives. As mentioned in the page on my professional involvements, I am also avidly involved in my community volunteering as an art museum tour guide.
